ASL GRAMMAR REFERENCE
=====================
This document defines the rules used to validate ASL gloss input.

---

## 1. WHAT IS ASL GLOSS?
ASL gloss is a written representation of American Sign Language using English words in ALL CAPS. Each word represents a single sign. Fingerspelled words are written with hyphens between letters: S-A-R-A-H.

---

## 2. SENTENCE STRUCTURE

### 2.1 Basic Word Order
ASL generally follows Topic-Comment structure, not English Subject-Verb-Object.

Valid:
  PIZZA ME LOVE          (Topic: pizza, Comment: I love it)
  DOG ME HAVE            (Topic: dog, Comment: I have one)
  ME FEEL HAPPY
  ME EAT BREAKFAST

### 2.2 Time Markers
Time expressions come at the START of a sentence.

Valid:
  YESTERDAY ME GO STORE
  TOMORROW ME WORK
  EVERY-DAY ME EAT EGG

Invalid:
  ME GO STORE YESTERDAY  (time marker at end is incorrect)

---

## 3. PRONOUNS

Use ME, YOU, HE, SHE, IT, WE, THEY — never I.

Valid:
  ME FEEL HAPPY
  ME LIVE NEW-YORK
  YOU WANT COFFEE

Invalid:
  I FEEL HAPPY           (never use I)
  I LIKE PIZZA           (never use I)

---

## 4. WH-QUESTIONS

WH-question words always go at the END of the sentence, or can be repeated at both start and end. They are never placed only at the beginning.

WH-question words: WHAT, WHERE, WHO, WHY, WHEN, HOW-MANY

Valid:
  YOUR NAME WHAT
  YOU LIVE WHERE
  YOU GO WHERE
  YOU FEEL HOW          (HOW used for feelings/manner)
  YOU WANT WHAT

Invalid:
  WHAT YOUR NAME        (WH-word at start only)
  WHERE YOU LIVE        (WH-word at start only)
  YOUR NAME WHERE       (WHERE used incorrectly for name — use WHAT)
  YOUR NAME HOW         (HOW used incorrectly for name — use WHAT)

---

## 5. YES/NO QUESTIONS

Yes/No questions use raised eyebrows (not marked in gloss) and typically mirror statement word order.

Valid:
  YOU HAVE DOG
  ME GO STORE YOU WANT
  YOU LIKE PIZZA

---

## 6. NEGATION

Negation is expressed with NOT placed after the verb, or with negative signs like NEVER, NONE.

Valid:
  ME LIKE-NOT PIZZA
  ME NOT WANT GO
  ME NEVER EAT MEAT

---

## 7. ADJECTIVES

Adjectives come AFTER the noun they describe.

Valid:
  CAT BLACK
  DOG BROWN
  HOUSE BIG
  ME LIKE COLOR BLUE DARK

Invalid:
  BLACK CAT             (adjective before noun is English order)

---

## 8. FINGERSPELLING

Fingerspelling is used for proper nouns (names, places) and words with no ASL sign. Written with letters separated by hyphens or spaces.

Valid:
  MY NAME S-A-R-A-H
  MY NAME S A R A H
  ME LIVE N-Y-C
  ME GO C-V-S

---

## 9. TOPICS AND PRONOUNS WITH CLASSIFIERS

To refer to a person or object mentioned earlier, use IX (index/point) or simply re-use the noun.

Valid:
  DOG IX BROWN
  TEACHER IX NICE

---

## 10. COMMON SIGNS AND THEIR CORRECT USAGE

  HELLO       — greeting
  THANK-YOU   — expressing thanks
  SORRY       — apologizing
  PLEASE      — polite request
  YES / NO    — affirmation / negation
  WANT        — desire
  LIKE        — preference
  LOVE        — strong preference
  HATE        — strong dislike
  HAVE        — possession
  NEED        — necessity
  GO          — movement
  COME        — approach
  SEE         — vision
  KNOW        — knowledge
  THINK       — thought
  FEEL        — emotion
  LIVE        — residence
  WORK        — occupation
  EAT         — consuming food
  DRINK       — consuming liquid
  SLEEP       — rest
  WAKE-UP     — waking
  COOK        — preparing food
  SELF        — doing something alone / by oneself
  EVERY-DAY   — daily habit
  FAVORITE    — most preferred
  COLOR       — hue
  NAME        — identifier
  WHAT        — WH-question for identity/object
  WHERE       — WH-question for location
  WHO         — WH-question for person
  HOW         — WH-question for manner or feeling
  WHEN        — WH-question for time
  WHY         — WH-question for reason
  HOW-MANY    — WH-question for quantity

---

## 11. INVALID PATTERNS TO FLAG

- Using I instead of ME
- Placing WH-question words at the start of a sentence only
- Using WHERE or HOW for asking someone's name (use WHAT)
- Placing adjectives before nouns
- Placing time markers at the end of a sentence
- Random unconnected words with no grammatical structure

---

## 12. VALIDATION RULE SUMMARY

When checking ASL gloss:
1. Confirm pronouns use ME/YOU/HE/SHE, not I
2. Confirm WH-words are at the END
3. Confirm time markers are at the START
4. Confirm adjectives follow nouns
5. Accept fingerspelling in letter-space or hyphen format
6. Accept subject-verb, topic-comment, and classifier structures
7. Flag gloss that has no coherent grammatical structure
8. Flag gloss that uses the wrong WH-word (e.g. WHERE for a name)

## 13. SINGLE SIGN UTTERANCES

Single signs are valid gloss. Not every input needs to be a full sentence.

Valid:
  HELLO
  YES
  NO
  THANK-YOU
  SORRY
  PLEASE
